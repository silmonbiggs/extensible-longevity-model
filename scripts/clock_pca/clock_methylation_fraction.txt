==============================================================================
  METHYLATION FRACTION OF BIOLOGICAL AGE
  Across published multi-component aging clocks
==============================================================================

RATIONALE
----------------------------------------
The ELM's BioAge weights four aging hallmarks: methylation (0.30),
DNA damage (0.30), heteroplasmy (0.25), senescent cells (0.15).
How does this compare to published biological age composites?

METHOD
----------------------------------------
For each clock, map components to ELM-equivalent categories:
  epigenetic  — age-dependent chromatin/methylation remodeling
  damage      — DNA/mitochondrial/oxidative damage
  senescence  — SASP, cellular senescence, immune senescence
  inflammation — systemic inflammaging (overlaps senescence)
  metabolic   — glucose, lipid, adipokine dysregulation
  organ_decline — renal, hepatic, pulmonary function loss

Variance contribution = (|beta| x population SD)^2 for weighted clocks,
or equal counts for DunedinPACE (uniform weights).
